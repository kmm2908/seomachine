## Core Services

### 1. Traditional Thai Massage
- **What it is**: Full-body treatment performed fully clothed on a floor mat. Uses acupressure, assisted stretching, and compression along the body's sen energy lines.
- **Duration**: 60 or 90 minutes (confirm with client)
- **Price range**: £55–£80
- **Key benefits**: Releases deep muscle tension, improves flexibility and joint mobility, relieves backache and sciatica, reduces stress
- **Ideal for**: Anyone seeking authentic Thai technique; first-timers; desk workers; those with chronic tension or poor flexibility

### 2. Thai Oil Massage
- **What it is**: Therapeutic massage using heated oils. Combines flowing strokes with targeted pressure work. Client undresses to underwear; privacy maintained throughout.
- **Key benefits**: Deep relaxation, improved circulation, skin nourishment, muscle recovery
- **Ideal for**: Those who prefer oil-based work; people wanting deep relaxation alongside physical benefit

### 3. Thai Aromatherapy Massage
- **What it is**: Oil massage using therapeutic-grade essential oils selected for their properties (relaxing, energising, or restorative)
- **Key benefits**: All oil massage benefits plus mood and nervous system support from aromatherapy
- **Ideal for**: Stress-related conditions, anxiety, mood support, general wellbeing

### 4. Thai Sports Massage
- **What it is**: Targeted treatment for active clients. Addresses compensation patterns, aids recovery between training sessions, and supports injury prevention.
- **Key benefits**: Faster recovery, improved range of motion, reduced injury risk, better performance
- **Ideal for**: Runners, gym-goers, cyclists, team sport players, anyone with a physically demanding lifestyle

### 5. Thai Facial Massage
- **What it is**: Focus on face, head, and neck. Releases tension in jaw, temples, scalp, and upper neck.
- **Key benefits**: Tension headache relief, jaw tension, improved circulation to face, deep relaxation
- **Ideal for**: Headache and migraine sufferers; jaw clenchers; high-stress clients

### 6. Thai Foot Massage
- **What it is**: Reflexology-based treatment covering feet and lower legs. Works reflex points that correspond to organs and body systems.
- **Key benefits**: Stimulates circulation, relieves foot fatigue, promotes full-body relaxation
- **Ideal for**: People on their feet all day; those who want a shorter treatment; clients seeking reflexology benefits

### 7. Thai Head Massage
- **What it is**: Targets scalp, neck, and upper shoulders. Can be done seated or lying down.
- **Key benefits**: Relieves tension headaches, neck and shoulder pain, improves circulation to scalp
- **Ideal for**: Office workers; headache sufferers; anyone with upper body tension

### 8. Thai Stone Massage
- **What it is**: Hot basalt stones used in combination with Thai massage techniques. Heat penetrates deeper into muscle tissue.
- **Key benefits**: Deeper muscle release, improved circulation, profound relaxation
- **Ideal for**: Chronic muscle tightness; those who want deeper work without intense pressure

## Content Creation Guidelines

When writing about Glasgow Thai Massage:

1. **Lead with the condition or benefit**, not the technique: "If your neck has been stiff for weeks, here's what's actually happening — and how Thai massage addresses it."
2. **Name the treatment specifically**: "Thai Sports Massage" not just "massage"
3. **Reference the Wat Pho training** when establishing credibility — it is a genuine differentiator
4. **Use the practitioners' names** (Maliwan, Jariya) in trust signal sections — they are the business
5. **Tie treatments to real life scenarios**: desk workers, athletes, parents, shift workers — the people who actually book
6. **Never invent conditions treated or claims about outcomes** that aren't supported by standard massage therapy evidence
