What Is Diabetic Neuropathy

Diabetic neuropathy is a group of nerve disorders caused by long-term high blood sugar. It is the most common complication of diabetes. Over time, high blood sugar damages the small vessels that carry oxygen to your nerves. This harms nerve function and can destroy nerve fibres entirely.

The most common form is peripheral neuropathy. It follows a glove-and-stocking pattern: numbness, burning, tingling, and sharp pain starting in the feet and lower legs, sometimes spreading to the hands.

According to the National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases, nerve damage of this kind affects nearly half of all people with diabetes. Symptoms are often worst at night. They can range from a dull ache to sharp, electric jolts.

Loss of sensation is equally serious. Many people injure their feet without realising it, because the pain signal has been lost.

How Massage Helps Diabetic Neuropathy and Nerve Pain

Massage helps diabetic neuropathy in two key ways: it improves local blood flow and it wakes up sensory nerve pathways. When nerves are starved of circulation, their function gets worse. Focused work on the feet and lower legs brings more blood to damaged nerve tissue. This delivers oxygen and nutrients, helping to restore feeling.

A randomised controlled trial on Thai foot massage and diabetic peripheral neuropathy found clear improvements in foot sensation, range of motion, and balance. Those who received regular Thai foot massage did better than the control group.

A separate systematic review on PubMed also found Thai foot massage among the approaches showing real benefits for symptom management. The reason is well understood. Stimulating sensory receptors in the skin and soft tissue triggers nerve pathways that cut pain. It also helps the nervous system re-engage with areas that have gone numb or too sensitive.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is massage safe if I have diabetic neuropathy?

For most people with diabetic neuropathy, massage is safe and well-tolerated when delivered by an experienced therapist. The key issue is reduced feeling in affected areas. Your therapist needs to know where sensation is lower so pressure can be managed with care.

At Glasgow Thai Massage, we take a full case history before every session and adapt the treatment to suit you. If you have open wounds, active foot ulcers, or very fragile skin, please consult your GP before booking.

Can Thai foot massage help with the burning and tingling in my feet?

Yes. Thai foot massage works by boosting circulation and stimulating sensory nerve pathways in the foot and lower leg. This can reduce the burning, tingling, and sensitivity linked to peripheral neuropathy. A randomised controlled trial found clear improvements in foot sensation and range of motion in diabetic patients who received Thai foot massage.

Results tend to be better with a regular course of sessions rather than a single treatment.

How often should I have massage for diabetic nerve pain?

Research points to regular sessions producing the most benefit. For most clients managing diabetic neuropathy, a session every two to four weeks is a good starting point. Your therapist at Glasgow Thai Massage can advise on how often to come based on how your symptoms respond.

The goal is to make massage a steady part of your self-care routine, not a one-off appointment.

Will massage cure my diabetic neuropathy?

Massage is not a cure for diabetic neuropathy, and we would never suggest otherwise. Nerve damage caused by diabetes is a long-term condition. It is best managed alongside your medical team.

What massage can do is reduce symptom severity, improve circulation to affected nerves, ease pain, and support better balance and movement. It works alongside medical treatment, not instead of it.

Are there any types of massage I should avoid with diabetic neuropathy?

Very deep, high-pressure work over areas of reduced sensation carries some risk. You may not be able to feel if the pressure is too much. This is why it matters to see a therapist who understands the condition and adjusts their approach.

At Glasgow Thai Massage, we manage pressure carefully throughout and give extra care in areas where feeling is reduced. We do not recommend self-applied deep pressure tools in affected areas without professional guidance.

Can massage help with the poor balance that comes with diabetic neuropathy?

Yes. Research has shown that Thai foot massage can improve balance in people with diabetic peripheral neuropathy. Better sensation and circulation in the feet gives the body more accurate signals about position. Balance depends on nerve input from the feet and lower legs.

When those signals improve, balance tends to improve with them. This is a useful benefit for older adults at risk of falls.
