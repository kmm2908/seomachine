Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Thai aromatherapy massage and this aromatherapy deep tissue oil massage?

Our standard Thai aromatherapy massage uses flowing strokes and moderate pressure alongside essential oils. It focuses on overall relaxation and mood. The aromatherapy deep tissue oil massage uses the same oils but applies much more sustained pressure. This reaches the deeper layers of muscle and connective tissue. It suits clients with ongoing tension, chronic knots, or muscle pain that needs direct, focused work.

Do I need to undress for this treatment?

Yes. Warm oil is applied directly to the skin, so you will undress to your comfort level. A towel covers you throughout. Only the area being worked on is uncovered at any time, so your comfort and modesty are always maintained.

How long is a session and how will I feel afterwards?

Sessions run for 60 or 90 minutes depending on what you book. Most clients feel deeply relaxed and physically lighter afterwards. Some notice mild muscle soreness where deep pressure was applied — similar to the feeling after a good workout. This usually settles within 24 to 48 hours. It is a normal part of the muscle release process.

Can I choose which essential oils are used?

Yes. Jariya will discuss your preferences and any sensitivities before the session. She will suggest a blend to suit your needs. If you have known allergies or skin reactions to specific oils, please mention these when you book or on arrival. Your safety and comfort always come first.

How often should I come for this treatment?

For chronic tension or muscle pain, sessions every one to two weeks work best to start. Once things have improved, many clients move to a monthly maintenance session. Jariya keeps session notes so each appointment builds on the progress made in the last.

Is this massage suitable if I have a specific injury or medical condition?

Please let us know about any injuries, medical conditions, or areas to avoid when you get in touch or at your consultation. Deep tissue work is not suitable over acutely inflamed areas or recent injuries. Jariya will adapt the treatment or suggest an alternative such as Thai sports massage if deep tissue pressure is not right for you at that time.
