Thai Deep Tissue Oil Massage in Greenock

If you carry chronic muscle tension, persistent back pain, or the kind of deep knots that regular massage never quite reaches, a Thai deep tissue oil massage in Greenock could be exactly what your body needs. Thai deep tissue oil massage applies firm, sustained pressure to the deeper layers of muscle and connective tissue. Warm oil is used throughout, allowing the therapist to move smoothly across the body. The oil cuts surface friction, making it easy to shift between broad flowing strokes and close work on tight spots.

Techniques include slow strokes along muscle fibres, acupressure on key points along the sen energy lines of traditional Thai practice, and direct work on chronic knots in the shoulders, neck, lower back, and hips. The result is a treatment that tackles real physical problems rather than just offering surface-level relief. The oil also feeds the skin and warms the tissue, making deeper pressure both more effective and more comfortable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to undress for a Thai deep tissue oil massage?

Yes, because oil is applied directly to the skin, you will undress for this treatment. You undress to your comfort level and are fully draped with towels throughout. Only the area being worked on is uncovered at any time. Your comfort and modesty are respected at every stage.

How long does a session last?

Sessions are available from 60 to 90 minutes. For a first visit targeting one problem area — such as a tight lower back or chronic shoulder tension — 60 minutes is a solid starting point. If you want full-body work with more time on multiple areas, 90 minutes is the better choice.

Will I be sore afterwards?

Some mild tenderness in the worked areas is normal. It usually settles within 24 to 48 hours. Drinking plenty of water after your session helps the body clear waste released from the tissue. Most clients find the soreness fades quickly and is followed by a clear sense of relief in the areas that were holding tension.

How often should I come for this treatment?

For chronic tension or recurring pain, sessions spaced one to two weeks apart tend to give the best results. As your body responds and tension reduces, you can move to monthly maintenance sessions. Jariya will advise on the right frequency based on what she finds during your first treatment.

What is the difference between Thai deep tissue oil massage and a standard Thai oil massage?

A standard Thai oil massage uses flowing strokes and moderate pressure, with the focus on relaxation and general tension relief. The deep tissue version applies much more focused pressure into the deeper layers of muscle and fascia. It targets chronic knots and structural tightness that lighter work cannot reach. It is a more intensive treatment aimed at fixing specific physical problems rather than general unwinding.

Is it suitable for someone who has never had a massage before?

It can be, provided you are comfortable with firm pressure. If you are new to massage and unsure, a Thai oil massage at moderate pressure is a good place to start before moving to deeper work.
