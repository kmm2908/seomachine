How Often Should You Get a Thai Massage? A Practical Guide for Every Lifestyle

It's one of the most common questions we hear at Glasgow Thai Massage: "I loved my last session — how soon should I come back?" The honest answer is that there's no single rule that applies to everyone.

The right frequency depends on why you're coming, what your body needs, and how you want to feel. This guide breaks it down clearly so you can build a routine that works for you.

For General Wellbeing and Stress Relief: Once a Month

If you're mainly using massage to unwind and keep your body balanced, once a month is a solid place to start. Monthly traditional Thai massage gives your nervous system a regular reset. It helps release the low-level tension that builds up from desk work and daily life.

It also keeps your flexibility from quietly fading over time.

Think of it the way you'd think about exercise. One good run doesn't transform your fitness. But a run every few weeks? Over time, that adds up.

The same is true for bodywork. A monthly session at Glasgow Thai Massage is enough to notice a real difference in how you carry yourself and how well you sleep.

You can book your session online and lock in a regular slot that fits your schedule.

For Desk Workers and Office Professionals: Every Two to Three Weeks

If you spend most of your day at a screen, your neck, shoulders, and lower back are taking more strain than you might think. Poor desk posture compresses the spine and shortens the hip flexors. It also creates tight, persistent tension across the upper back — the kind that doesn't fully release overnight.

For anyone working long hours in Glasgow's offices, coming in every two to three weeks makes a clear difference. At that rate, a skilled therapist can work through tension before it becomes a chronic problem.

Thai massage works well here because it combines acupressure with assisted stretching. It targets both the tight muscles and the stiff joints that desk work creates.

Many of our regular clients work around Blythswood Square and the St Vincent Street corridor. A fortnightly session fits naturally into their week. It's one hour that pays back several times over in how you feel for the days that follow.

For Active People and Athletes: Weekly or Bi-Weekly

If you train regularly — running, cycling, gym work, or team sport — your muscles are under repeated stress. Recovery is where progress actually happens. Massage is one of the most effective tools to support it.

Weekly or bi-weekly Thai sports massage focuses on muscle recovery, range of motion, and injury prevention. At this rate, the work becomes truly restorative. You're not just easing soreness after the fact.

You're keeping the muscle balance and joint mobility that stops injuries from developing.

Research backs this up. Studies on neck pain have found that more frequent sessions over a short period work better than the same hours spread out. The same logic applies to sports recovery — regular, well-spaced sessions build on each other.

For Chronic Pain and Ongoing Conditions: Start Weekly, Then Review

For anyone managing chronic back pain, sciatica, or persistent tightness, the approach is different. In the early stages, weekly sessions give the body the steady input it needs to start shifting out of a pain pattern. Many practitioners suggest starting at once or twice a week, then easing back as symptoms improve.

Massage for lower back pain works best as part of a sustained plan, not a one-off. A single deep-tissue session can bring real relief. But it takes repeated work to address the underlying muscle problems and postural habits that caused the issue.

Once things settle, you can move to a maintenance schedule of every two to four weeks.

If you're not sure what frequency suits your situation, raise it with your therapist at the start of your first session. A good therapist will tailor their advice to what they find — not give you a generic answer.

Signs You're Leaving It Too Long Between Sessions

Your body sends reliable signals when it's been too long. Tension headaches that creep in mid-week. Stiffness when you get up in the morning.

That familiar tightness across the tops of your shoulders that never quite goes away. Broken sleep is another sign. When muscles stay contracted, they stop the nervous system from fully switching off at night.

If these sound familiar, your current schedule probably isn't frequent enough. Try shortening the gap for a month or two and notice how you feel. Most people find a natural rhythm once they experience how different regular massage feels compared to occasional treatment.

The right frequency is the one that keeps you feeling well — not the one you fall back on when things get bad. Whether that's monthly upkeep or weekly recovery, Glasgow Thai Massage is easy to reach from anywhere in the city centre. Booking online takes less than two minutes.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should you get a massage for the first time?

If you've never had a professional massage before, there's no need to commit to a set schedule straight away. A good starting point is one session, then waiting a week or two to see how your body responds.

Some people feel temporary muscle soreness after their first session — especially after deep tissue or traditional Thai massage. Once you know how you respond, you can choose an interval that suits your lifestyle and goals.

Can you get a massage too often?

For most people, more than twice a week over a long period would be too much. Your body needs time to process the work done in each session. Going too often can leave tissues feeling worn out rather than restored.

That said, during injury recovery, more frequent sessions under professional guidance can be the right call. Your therapist will let you know if they think you need more time between visits.

Is monthly Thai massage enough to see real benefits?

Yes. A monthly Thai massage is enough to notice real benefits — especially for stress, flexibility, and general muscle health. The key is staying consistent over time.

One session brings quick relief. But the effect of regular monthly sessions over three to six months is much greater. If you have a specific condition or higher physical demands, you may need to come in more often. For most people, though, monthly is a strong and sustainable starting point.

Does massage frequency differ depending on the type of massage?

Yes, it can. Traditional Thai massage uses assisted stretching and acupressure along the body's sen energy lines. It works the whole system, which makes it well suited to regular visits.

Deep tissue and sports massage use more intense pressure on specific muscles. These may need a day or two of recovery, so weekly sessions suit active people more than those using massage purely to relax. A lighter treatment like Thai oil or aromatherapy massage can be enjoyed more often without issue.
