# AI Brand Visibility — Getting Cited by LLMs

Source: Brian Dean (Backlinko / Exploding Topics), YouTube
URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JHxMyS7TaS8

---

## Core Principle

AI tools don't rank pages the way Google does. They choose which brands, tools, and sources to surface based on signals that happen **off your site** — third-party mentions, community discussions, and repeated consistent positioning. Getting into AI answers is a **content and distribution strategy problem, not a technical one**.

---

## Strategy 1 — Get Listed on "Best Of" and Comparison Pages

These pages are primed for LLM training: they rank, get traffic, and are frequently cited as sources in AI answers.

**Target page types:**
- "Best [tools/services] for X"
- "Top platforms for Y"
- "[Brand A] vs [Brand B]" comparisons
- "[Competitor] alternatives"

**How:**
1. Find existing pages — search Google or prompt LLMs with "best of" questions and note which pages they cite. Those are the ideal targets.
2. Reach out to the author with a brief, factual, non-hyped note: describe what your product/service is and who it's for.
3. Focus on getting the right **description**, even if the link is no-follow or absent.
4. Bonus: flag outdated or broken resources on their page and offer yours as a replacement.

**Also create your own** "best of" list on your site — but LLMs need to see the association confirmed on third-party sites too.

---

## Strategy 2 — Show Up Where People Ask for Recommendations

Reddit threads, Quora questions, niche forums and communities — AI systems learn from the answers in these discussions.

**Rules:**
- Only reply to high-intent, specific questions where someone is looking for exactly what you sell. Skip vague or theoretical threads.
- Keep answers grounded, non-hyped: explain what you use, why, when it makes sense, and when it doesn't. Sound like a user, not a salesperson.
- **Use the same or similar description of your brand every time.** Consistent phrasing helps LLMs make the connection between your brand and the problem you solve.

---

## Strategy 3 — Press Releases for Consistent Positioning

Press releases usually won't get picked up widely, but that's not the point for AI SEO. Their value is:

- Forces you to write a clear, neutral description of what your brand does and who it's for.
- That same wording gets reused everywhere: website, outreach emails, Reddit answers, journalist pitches.

The press release itself isn't a strong signal. The **repeated use of the same description across multiple online locations** is.

---

## Strategy 4 — Build a Content Machine (Most Important)

Quoted from Mike King: *"Winning AI search is fundamentally a content strategy problem, not a technical problem."*

LLM.txt, chunking, and markup won't substitute for being a brand that people naturally talk about. The goal is to get your brand mentioned **automatically and at scale**.

**How:**
1. Pick one main platform (blog, YouTube, newsletter, social) and publish consistently — value first, pitch second.
2. Redistribute that content across other platforms (blog post → YouTube video → X thread → email newsletter). One piece of content becomes 5–50 over time.
3. Consider a free tier with no login barrier — accelerates Product Hunt features and organic mentions.
4. A newsletter keeps your brand top of mind for writers and creators who may reference you.

The model: **create once, distribute forever.** Manual "best of" outreach and Reddit comments don't scale. A content machine generates mentions 24/7 without ongoing manual effort, and AI tools recognise this as more natural.

## Key Takeaway for This Project

For local service clients (GTM, SDY), the LLM visibility playbook scales down as follows:

- **Get listed on local "best massage" / "best Thai massage Glasgow" pages** — directories, roundups, local blog posts, visit Glasgow guides.
- **Encourage clients or the agency to respond in local Reddit/Facebook/community threads** recommending the business — consistently use the same description.
- **Use a consistent brand description** across Google Business Profile, all directory listings, and website copy — repetition builds LLM association.
- **Content machine equivalent** — the blog subdomain (GTB) publishing regular topical content is exactly this. Each post reinforces the brand-keyword association.
