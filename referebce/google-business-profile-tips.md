## Tip 1: Put Text on Your Photos (and Add Videos)

**The problem:** Abstract photos don't tell Google what your business does. An AI reading a photo of "lawyers at a table" sees people in suits, not a personal injury law firm.

**The fix:**
1. Drop your photo into an AI chat and ask it to describe the image
2. Note what keywords are missing
3. Open in Canva or Photoshop and add a small caption at the bottom (e.g. *"Personal injury law firm Denver — discussing a car accident case with clients"*)
4. Re-upload the annotated photo

Google's vision AI will now extract that text and have the keyword context it needs.

**Also: Upload videos**
- Videos autoplay in Google Maps results — they stop the scroll and drive engagement
- Worth far more than static photos for grabbing attention

## Tip 2: Name Your Profile in Multiple Languages

Almost no businesses do this, making it a strong competitive differentiator.

**How to do it:**
1. Open Google Maps and change the interface language (via the menu → Language)
2. Find your business listing
3. Click **Suggest an edit** → Name
4. Enter the translated business name
5. Repeat for each language relevant to your area

Useful anywhere multilingual audiences exist (tourists + locals, border regions, cities with large immigrant communities).

## Tip 3: Connect Your Social Profiles

Google is now pulling Instagram/TikTok post content as snippets directly into Maps results — but **only if your social profiles are linked**.

**How to connect:**
1. Go to your Google Business Profile
2. Click **Edit Profile**
3. Scroll to the **Social profiles** section
4. Click the pencil icon and add all your social accounts

Google reads everything you post on social to better understand your business. Linking ensures you get credit for that content.

## Tip 4: Open a Real Office in Your Target Market

**The problem:** Google Maps rankings are heavily proximity-based. If you're 30 miles away, you're unlikely to rank in another town.

**The solution:** Rent a small, inexpensive physical office in your target city.

- Search Craigslist for office/desk space in the target area (~$450/mo is viable)
- Create a new Google Business Profile at that address
- Must have proper signage to comply with Google guidelines

**Do NOT use:**
- Virtual offices
- PO boxes
- UPS Store mailboxes
- Any mailbox service

These violate Google's guidelines and result in suspended listings.

**Important:** When setting the service area for the new location, make sure it does **not overlap** with your existing location's service area — overlapping areas is a common cause of suspension.

## Tip 5: Change Your Primary Category Seasonally

**Primary GBP category is the #1 local search ranking factor** (per the Local Search Ranking Factor survey).

If your business offers seasonal services, switch your primary category to match the current season.

**Example:** A lawn care company that also does snow removal should switch their primary category to *"Snow Removal Service"* during winter months.

**How to change it:**
1. Go to your Google Business Profile
2. Click **Edit Profile**
3. Click **Categories**
4. Update the primary category
5. Click **Save**

## Tip 6: Level Up Google Posts + Post as a Customer

### Part A: Use Multi-Media Posts

Stop posting single static images. Google gives you space for multiple files — use it.

- Drag in multiple videos **and** multiple photos in a single post
- Create short MP4 videos easily using Canva templates with text overlays
- Mixed media posts are far more eye-catching and engaging

### Part B: Post on Your Own Profile as a Customer

Almost no business owners know this is possible.

**How to do it:**
1. Find your business on **Google Maps** (as a regular user, not owner)
2. Scroll down to the **"Add photos and videos"** section
3. Add a post with text and/or video as a customer

This appears in a prominent **"Updates from customers"** section on your profile and persists for months. It gives you additional content real estate that your competitors aren't using.
